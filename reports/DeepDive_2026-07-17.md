## The finding that changes everything: sargassum

2026 is the **second-worst seaweed year ever recorded** (USF Optical Oceanography Lab, June 30 bulletin: 33.6M metric tons; Caribbean at record June levels, beaching "will continue and likely increase" through August).

- **Hit zones (avoid for clear water):** Cancun Hotel Zone (clarity ~5/10 in Aug), Riviera Maya/Playa del Carmen (~4.5), **Punta Cana/Bávaro (~4 — worst on the list)**, Barbados ("one of the worst years on record"), Puerto Rico (state of emergency extended).
- **Protected zones:** Cozumel west coast (9/10 — the island physically blocks the flow), Grace Bay TCI (9.5), Seven Mile Beach Cayman (9), Negril (8 — historically seaweed-free), Palm Beach Aruba (8 — outside the belt entirely), Costa Mujeres (7.5 — NW-facing, light in July reports).

**Hurricanes: green light.** CSU's July update forecasts the quietest season since 2014 (9 named storms vs 14 avg; El Niño shear). NOAA agrees (55% below-normal). Aug 8–15 also sits before the climatological ramp (peak ~Sep 10). Aruba remains the only zero-risk option (no direct hit since 1851).

## The recommendation

**Book: Riu Latino, Costa Mujeres — $2,412 for both of you.** It wins on every axis that matters to you: cheapest adults-only anywhere, 3h45 **nonstop** from DTW, new resort (2024), and its NW-facing beach is one of the few mainland-Mexico stretches staying relatively clear in a record seaweed year. Aug 8 start confirmed cheapest.

**If crystal water is non-negotiable** (and it sounded like it is): **Secrets Aura Cozumel, $3,568** — adults-only 5★ with swim-out suites on the west coast of Cozumel, which the seaweed physically cannot reach. The +$1,156 buys certainty. Flight is ~5.5h with one stop instead of a nonstop.

**The honest caveat on the old favorites:** Secrets The Vine and everything in Punta Cana are still great resorts at great prices — but in August 2026 you'd be gambling the "crystal clear" part of this trip on which way the wind blows that week. Resorts rake sand daily; they can't rake the water.

Caveats: beach-level sargassum is wind-dependent day to day (USF disclaims beach-level prediction); flight durations for 1-stop destinations vary by connection — verify the actual itinerary CheapCaribbean picks before paying. Prices are quotes and move.
