The pick — crystal water, guaranteed

Secrets Aura Cozumel adults-only

Cozumel west coast · 5★ · swim-out suites
$3,568 total for two

Cozumel's west coast is physically shielded from the sargassum flow — clarity there is a near-certainty, not a bet, even in a record seaweed year. Adults-only, swim-out suites, and the price has held steady across every verification pass. The trade: a ~5.5h one-stop flight instead of a nonstop.

The budget alternative

Riu Latino adults-only

Costa Mujeres, Mexico · 4.5★ · opened 2024
$2,412 total for two

Cheapest adults-only anywhere in the sweep and a 3h45m nonstop from DTW — the save-$1,156 option if we'd rather bank the difference. Its northwest-facing beach is one of the few mainland stretches expected to stay relatively clear, but "relatively" is doing some work there.

The hedge: fully refundable Riu Palace Aruba/Antillas packages stay on watch as a fallback — Palm Beach scores 8/10 water even this year, it's just 2.5× the price in August. And the honest caveat on everywhere else: resorts rake sand daily; they can't rake the water.

August conditions — the finding that changed the ranking

2026 is the second-worst sargassum (seaweed) year ever recorded — 33.6M metric tons, per the USF Optical Oceanography Lab's June 30 bulletin, with beaching likely to increase through August. Hurricanes, by contrast, are a green light: CSU forecasts the quietest season since 2014, and Aug 8–15 sits before the climatological ramp.
